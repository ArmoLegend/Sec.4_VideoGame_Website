Sid Meier's Civilization VI Review

85/100

Good Points

  • Engaging and addictive gameplay.
  • Large amount of content continuously being added.
  • Legendary and amazingly diverse soundtrack.
  • Plenty of single-player and multi-player game modes.

Bad Points

  • Computer players can be pretty infuriating at times.
  • Expensive DLC on top of the base game.

Review

For nearly 30 years, the Sid Meier's Civilization series has been defining the strategy game genre and for good reason. The thought of building your own nation from ancient times to the modern day and beyond while competing with others for world domination is an interesting idea and translates beautifully as a video game. By far one of the series' best points is just how much content has been added since it's beginning in 1991, whether it be new playable civilizations, completely redesigning previous features or even making the game's presentation more updated, every Civilization game feels way different and improved than the last. Civilization VI continues this trend and many find it to work amazingly. Not a single playable civilization seems out of place here, whether you're trying to enforce naval supremacy as the Kongo or charging into Rome on your war elephant while being commanded by a blood-thirsty Mahatma Gandhi, this game really allows for creativity in how you plan to shape your empire for the future. But say taking over the world isn't on your to-do-list today, so instead you decide to colonize another solar system or make your belief of the great chicken gods the world religion again highlighting the flexibility you have as a player to win a game.
